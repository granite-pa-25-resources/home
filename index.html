Overview

This portal provides an independent, in-depth look at data and trends that are essential to understanding the recent Population Advisory Committee (PAC) recommendations and their near and long-term implications for Granite School District‚Äôs Area 5 elementary schools.

  • Enrollment Trends: 21-year histories + multi-method statistical forecasts for all 10 Area 5 schools.
  • Student Mobility: 2024-2025 analysis of where Area 5 students live vs. where they attend; reveals true demand and choice behavior across 3,521 boundary students and 4,226 school enrollments.

All data sourced from district records and PAC documents.

Why This Portal Matters

Accessible data drives informed decisions. This portal transforms complex district datasets into interactive visualizations you can explore, empowering parents, educators, and community members to engage meaningfully in the boundary study process.

  • 21-Year Enrollment Perspective:
    Interactive charts reveal that while Area 5 enrollment has dropped overall, individual school trajectories vary dramatically. Statistical forecasts with confidence intervals help assess capacity planning assumptions.
    Explore school-by-school trends & forecasts ‚Üí
  • Student Movement Transparency:
    Sankey flow diagrams and mobility matrices make complex enrollment patterns instantly visible: which schools draw students from across boundaries (Morningside 66.3%, Penn 55.0%), and which retain local families (Cottonwood 83.5%). These data are critical for understanding how boundary changes will impact real families.
    See interactive mobility visualizations ‚Üí
  • Program Impact Analysis:
    Data-driven examination of how specialized programs (ALC, DLI) shape enrollment. For example, understanding that Morningside's traditional pathway serves as the primary 3rd grade ALC entry point helps assess the true impact of magnet conversion.
    Dive into the program analysis ‚Üí
  • Evidence-Based Planning:
    By making district data accessible and analyzable, this portal enables stakeholders to ask better questions, identify unintended consequences (like Eastwood's 53.2% choice population), and advocate for solutions grounded in evidence rather than assumptions.

Our Goal: Democratize access to PAC data so every voice in the conversation can be informed by the same facts, trends, and insights that guide district planning.
